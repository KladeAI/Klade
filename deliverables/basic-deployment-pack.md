## 4. Security & Compliance FAQ

**What data does Klade store?**
Standard deployments use exclusively public/government data (SEC EDGAR, FRED, FDIC, U.S. Treasury). No client PII or proprietary data is stored on Klade servers unless you opt in on-prem.

**Is your infrastructure SOC 2 compliant?**
Yes. Our database layer runs on Supabase Pro with SOC 2 Type II-certified infrastructure, enterprise-grade encryption at rest and in transit, and role-based access controls with full audit logging.

**What about proprietary client data?**
On-prem deployments keep all proprietary data within your network boundary. It never touches Klade cloud.

**Does Clay give investment advice?**
No. Clay is a research and analysis tool. It does not make buy/sell recommendations or provide personalized investment advice. It supports professional judgment — it does not replace it.

**How do you handle citations?**
Citation-first: every data point is traceable to its source (filing, date, section). Clay is explicit about data freshness and will flag when information may be stale rather than fabricate numbers.

**What actions require approval?**
Sending external emails, editing external-facing calendar events, sharing documents outside your tenant, and any payment or financial execution action all require explicit human approval. All actions are audit-logged per tenant.
