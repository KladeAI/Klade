The Basics

Just talk normally. Clay understands plain English. You don't need special formatting, code words, or technical jargon. Ask Clay the way you'd ask a colleague sitting across the desk.

Examples:
> "What happened with Apple's earnings last quarter?"
> "Compare Tesla and Ford on margins and revenue growth"
> "Give me a quick macro snapshot — rates, inflation, where we stand"

How to Get Better Results

1. Tell Clay what you need it for

Context makes Clay sharper.

X "Tell me about interest rates"

> "I'm meeting with a client worried about rising rates — give me talking points on how rate hikes affect mid-cap growth stocks"

2. Specify the format

  • "Give me a quick take" → 2-3 sentence answer
  • "Walk me through this" → Detailed, structured analysis
  • "Make it a table" → Clean tabular format
  • "Draft it as an email" → Ready to copy and send
  • "Send me a PDF" → Formatted document delivered to chat

3. Name your audience

  • "Explain like I'm presenting to the board" → Executive-level, polished
  • "Give me the analyst-level detail" → Granular, with all the numbers
  • "Keep it simple" → Plain English, no jargon

4. Ask follow-ups

Clay remembers the conversation. After any analysis, keep going:

  • "Now compare that to their biggest competitor"
  • "What are the bear case risks?"
  • "Turn that into a client-ready memo"

5. Challenge Clay

Clay has conviction and respects pushback. Ask "Are you sure about that number?" and Clay will re-verify with the source citation.

What to Expect

  • Every number is cited. Filing name, date, and section included.
  • Clay admits what it doesn't know. No guessing — explicit about gaps.
  • Work arrives finished. No narration of process, just the deliverable.
  • It's fast. Most responses in seconds. Complex models ~1 minute.
