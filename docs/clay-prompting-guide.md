## How to Get Better Results

### 1. Tell Clay what you need it for
Context makes Clay sharper.

❌ "Tell me about interest rates"
✅ "I'm meeting with a client who's worried about rising rates — give me talking points on how rate hikes affect mid-cap growth stocks"

### 2. Specify the format you want
Clay adapts to what you need.

- **"Give me a quick take"** → 2-3 sentence answer
- **"Walk me through this"** → Detailed, structured analysis
- **"Make it a table"** → Clean tabular format
- **"Draft it as an email"** → Ready to copy and send
- **"Send me a PDF"** → Formatted document delivered to your chat

### 3. Name your audience
Clay adjusts tone and depth.

- **"Explain like I'm presenting to the board"** → Executive-level, polished
- **"Give me the analyst-level detail"** → Granular, with all the numbers
- **"Keep it simple, I'm not a finance person"** → Plain English, no jargon

### 4. Ask follow-ups
Clay remembers the conversation. After any analysis, just keep going:

- "Now compare that to their biggest competitor"
- "What are the bear case risks?"
- "Turn that into a client-ready memo"
- "Can you add a sensitivity table?"

### 5. Challenge Clay
Clay is built to have conviction, and it respects pushback:

- "I disagree — here's why..." → Clay will engage with your reasoning
- "What's the bull case against your view?" → Gets the other side
- "Are you sure about that number?" → Clay will re-verify and cite the source

## What to Expect from Clay

**Every number is cited.** When Clay gives you a financial figure, it tells you where it came from — the filing, the date, the section. If Clay can't cite a source, it will tell you the figure is estimated.

**Clay admits what it doesn't know.** If data is missing, stale, or uncertain, Clay says so directly instead of guessing.

**Work arrives finished.** You get the deliverable, not a narration of how it was built. No "Let me look that up..." or "Processing your request..." — just the answer.

**It's fast.** Most responses come back in seconds. Complex models and PDF reports may take a minute.
