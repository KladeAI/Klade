# David Fialkow — Mentorship Call Prep

> **Note:** Spelling is **Fialkow**, not Fialco. Don't get this wrong on the call.

## Who He Is (30-second cheat sheet)

- **Co-founder & Managing Director of General Catalyst** (~$43B AUM, 800+ portfolio companies)
- Built and sold **4 companies** before starting GC in 2000 — he's an operator-turned-investor, not a finance guy
- **Two-time Oscar-winning documentary producer** (*Icarus*, *Navalny*) — passion projects with his wife Nina
- Sits on **MIT's Board of Trustees**, chairs The Engine at MIT's investment committee
- Core belief: VCs should have **empathy for founders** and give them "permission to go for it"
- Values: **endurance over flash**, **storytelling as a founder skill**, patience for long-term value creation

## What GC Is Doing Right Now

- **Global Resilience** = GC's macro thesis. Five pillars: healthcare, defense, financial services, applied AI, manufacturing
- **$1.5B AI roll-up engine** — buying fragmented services businesses, injecting AI, doubling EBITDA margins in 12 months
- **Percepta** (launched late 2025) — deploys AI teams inside enterprises (healthcare, manufacturing, finance, gov). Partnered with AWS + Anthropic
- **Financial services is explicitly one of GC's five resilience pillars** — Klade sits directly in their worldview
- Portfolio includes: Anthropic, Stripe, Ramp, Anduril, Glean, Mercor, Windsurf

## 🎯 Conversation Topics That'll Land

1. **"AI replacing workflows, not just tasks" in financial services** — GC's roll-up strategy is literally about automating entire service categories (data entry, reasoning, customer service). Klade's vision of replacing the junior analyst workflow maps perfectly. Talk about how mid-market firms can't afford $200K analysts but need the same output quality.

2. **The resilience angle for mid-market finance** — GC's thesis says critical infrastructure needs modernization. Mid-market firms are critical to the economy but running on Excel and gut feel. Frame Klade as financial resilience infrastructure for the companies that actually make up most of GDP.

3. **Operator-to-founder instincts** — David built 4 companies before becoming a VC. He respects founders who think about unit economics, not just TAM slides. Talk about how you're building for revenue from day one, not playing the fundraise game.

4. **AI adoption curves in conservative industries** — GC's own research shows portfolio companies are increasing AI spend 1.5x every 6 months. But finance is lagging. Talk about why that gap = opportunity, and what it takes to sell AI to a CFO who still prints spreadsheets.

## 💬 One-Liners to Drop Naturally

- *"The interesting thing about mid-market finance is that these firms generate billions in aggregate but individually can't afford the tools that Goldman has. That's not a cost problem — it's an architecture problem."*

- *"We're not building a chatbot that answers finance questions. We're building the analyst that shows up at 6 AM, already has the model built, and asks the partner what they want to change."*

- *"I keep thinking about GC's roll-up thesis — you're buying services businesses and injecting AI to double margins. We're doing the same thing, except the customer keeps the business and we provide the AI layer."*

- *"The hardest part isn't the AI. It's earning trust from a 55-year-old CFO who's been doing this for 30 years. That's a go-to-market problem, not a tech problem."*

- *"Most AI finance tools are built by engineers who've never sat in a deal room. We're building with the people who actually do the work."*

## ❓ Smart Questions for David

1. **"GC launched Percepta to embed AI teams inside enterprises. What made you decide to build that in-house rather than back a startup doing the same thing — and what does that tell you about where the value accrues in enterprise AI?"** *(Shows you've done homework on their latest moves, and gets at a strategic question about build vs. buy that's relevant to Klade's positioning.)*

2. **"You've said founders need endurance over flash. For a company selling AI into a conservative industry like finance — how do you think about the balance between moving fast on product and moving slow enough to build trust with buyers?"** *(Directly references his philosophy, and it's a genuine tension Klade faces.)*

3. **"Financial services is one of GC's five resilience pillars. Where do you see the biggest unlock — is it the infrastructure layer, the workflow layer, or the decision-intelligence layer?"** *(Gets him talking about where he sees opportunity, which tells you where Klade should position.)*
