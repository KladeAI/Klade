## SECTION 1: Remote/Virtual Accelerators & Incubators

### 1. LAUNCH Accelerator
- **Investment:** $125,000 upfront + option to invest $250K in next 2 rounds
- **Equity:** ~6% (standard accelerator terms)
- **Format:** 14-week hybrid program; remote participation possible
- **Deadline:** Cohort 36 started March 2026; next cohort applications likely open soon — apply now at launchaccelerator.co
- **Requirements:** Must have MVP/prototype with early metrics (not just an idea). Very selective — 7 startups per cohort
- **Speed:** ~2-3 months from acceptance to funding
- **URL:** https://launchaccelerator.co
- **Klade fit:** Strong — you have a working product with testers. B2B enterprise AI is their sweet spot. Jason Calacanis's network is excellent for enterprise sales intros. Remote-friendly.

### 2. 500 Global
- **Investment:** $150,000
- **Equity:** ~5-6%
- **Format:** 4-month program, historically has remote/hybrid options
- **Deadline:** Rolling batches; check 500.co for next fintech cohort
- **Requirements:** Early-stage startup with clear market opportunity
- **Speed:** ~2-3 months from application to funding
- **URL:** https://500.co
- **Klade fit:** Strong fintech track record. Global network. Investment amount exceeds your minimum need. They've backed many AI/fintech companies.

### 3. MassChallenge
- **Investment:** Up to $100K in cash prizes (no investment — it's a competition)
- **Equity:** 0% — fully equity-free
- **Format:** 4-month accelerator, has run virtual/hybrid cohorts
- **Deadline:** Typically spring/summer applications; check masschallenge.org
- **Requirements:** Early-stage startup with social impact or innovation angle
- **Speed:** ~3-4 months (program duration + judging)
- **URL:** https://masschallenge.org
- **Klade fit:** Equity-free is huge. Fintech innovation track exists. Boston-based but has run remote cohorts. "AI replacing manual finance work" has a strong narrative for their judges.

### 4. Plug and Play Fintech
- **Investment:** No direct cash investment; provides corporate partner intros (Visa, JPMorgan, etc.)
- **Equity:** 0% (equity-free via corporate sponsorship model)
- **Format:** 3-month program, Silicon Valley-based but remote-friendly
- **Deadline:** Rolling batches throughout the year
- **Requirements:** Fintech startup with working product
- **Speed:** N/A for direct funding, but corporate pilot deals can lead to revenue
- **URL:** https://www.plugandplaytechcenter.com/fintech
- **Klade fit:** Not direct funding, BUT their corporate partners (banks, asset managers) are your exact customers. Could lead to pilot revenue faster than raising money. Worth applying alongside funding efforts.

### 5. Techstars (Other Programs Beyond NYC)

## SECTION 3: Non-Dilutive Grants

### 11. Ben Franklin Technology Partners (Southeastern PA)
- **Grant size:** $25,000 – $250,000 (varies by program)
- **Equity:** No equity — but has payback terms (repay from revenue, not dilutive)
- **Deadline:** Rolling — submit inquiry anytime
- **Application:** sep.benfranklin.org/entrepreneurs/how-to-apply/
- **Requirements:** Must be tech-focused, located in Philadelphia metro (Philly, Bucks, Chester, Delaware, Montgomery counties). Need technological differentiation and growth potential.
- **Speed:** 2-4 months (multi-stage review process)
- **URL:** https://sep.benfranklin.org
- **Klade fit:** ⭐ EXCELLENT. This is literally designed for Philadelphia tech startups. AI financial analyst software is exactly "tech-focused with substantial differentiation." Multiple sub-programs: StartupPHL Seed Fund II targets Philly seed-stage tech. Apply immediately.
- **Sub-programs to explore:**
  - StartupPHL Seed Fund II ($5M fund for Philly tech)
  - Digital Health Seed Fund (adjacent — finance health?)
  - General early-stage investment program

### 12. NSF I-Corps
- **Grant size:** $50,000
- **Equity:** 0%
- **Deadline:** Rolling — teams accepted on ongoing basis
- **Application:** Via NSF I-Corps website; need to find a local I-Corps Hub (Penn or Drexel likely have one)
- **Requirements:** Must participate in customer discovery curriculum. Need a technical lead, entrepreneurial lead, and industry mentor. Academic connection helpful.
- **Speed:** 3-6 months (program is ~7 weeks, funding comes during/after)
- **URL:** https://new.nsf.gov/funding/initiatives/i-corps
- **Klade fit:** Good. $50K non-dilutive. The customer discovery curriculum would actually help validate Klade's PMF with finance firms. Being connected to Haverford/Philadelphia academic ecosystem helps. Downside: slower process.

### 13. NSF SBIR Phase I
- **Grant size:** Up to $275,000
- **Equity:** 0%
- **Deadline:** Rolling — submit Project Pitch first, then invited to full proposal in a submission window
- **Application:** Research.gov → submit Project Pitch → ~30 days for feedback → if invited, submit full proposal
- **Requirements:** U.S.-based small business (<500 employees), 51%+ U.S.-owned. PI must be primarily employed by the company.
- **Speed:** 6-9 months (slowest option, but largest non-dilutive amount)
- **URL:** https://seedfund.nsf.gov
- **Klade fit:** Largest non-dilutive option. AI for financial services qualifies. Worth starting the Project Pitch process NOW even though money is slow — it can fund future development while other sources cover SOC 2 immediately.

### 14. FedEx Small Business Grant Contest
- **Grant size:** Up to $50,000 (grand prize) + $7,500 in services
- **Equity:** 0%
- **Deadline:** Spring 2026 (check fedex.com/grants for exact dates)
- **Requirements:** U.S.-based for-profit, <99 employees, FedEx shipping account open 6+ months before March 1, 2026
- **Speed:** 3-4 months (contest format with judging period)
- **URL:** https://www.fedex.com/en-us/small-business/grants.html
- **Klade fit:** Moderate. The $50K prize is perfect amount. BUT requires FedEx shipping account (probably don't have one). Contest format means uncertain outcome. Worth checking if you can quickly open a FedEx account and apply.
